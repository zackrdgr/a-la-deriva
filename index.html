The cruise port is a built-from-scratch village. We're heading to Krazy Lobster on the malecón for beachside massages and a long lounger afternoon.

★ Día III Anchor ★

Krazy Lobster · beach massages

Mahahual malecón. Beachside massage tables under palapas, drinks within reach, lounger time around it. Beach-club vibe (music, cruise crowd) rather than zen-spa silence. that's the trade-off, and we're choosing it.

El Plan

Some shape ideas

  • Off the boat is the move. The in-port "Maya Spa" kiosks are a chair-massage cattle call. skip them.
  • Getting there: taxi or colectivo south to the malecón (about ten to fifteen minutes, posted rate). Krazy Lobster is on the south end of the malecón.
  • A typical shape: massage under a palapa, lunch with feet in the sand, lounger time, slow drinks. Skip pieces if energy says so.
  • Heading back ninety minutes before all-aboard is the safe buffer. Mahahual taxis are reliable but not instant at peak return.
El Spa

What to ask for at Krazy Lobster

  • Massage tables sit under palapas right on the beach. Walk up to the bar and ask about massage. they'll set us up.
  • Standard menu tends to be Swedish, deep tissue, and Mayan-style.
  • Lounger + drinks usually included or added on. confirm the package on arrival.
El Mood

What we're walking into

  • Krazy Lobster's main identity is a beach bar. Music, drinks, casual party energy.
  • Massage experience here = treatment + bar vibes nearby. Not silent, not aromatherapy zen.
  • If anyone wants quieter mid-trip, Maya Chan (north of pier, books out) or Mahahual Spa (a block off the malecón) are the calm-and-traditional alternatives.

Food & drink on the malecón

Krazy Lobster has its own kitchen (seafood-leaning, named after the lobster). The malecón is a flat two-kilometer boardwalk if we want to wander between massages.

  • Krazy Lobster's own kitchen – seafood, lobster, cold beer right where we are.
  • Ki'Bok – ceviche-leaning, palapa-shaded tables, casual.
  • El Fenicio – fish tacos, more local than touristy.
  • 100% Agave on the north malecón – mezcal flights, owner who knows agaves.
  • Tropicante – longer hang with music, no hard sell.

If anyone wants something else entirely

The port village has a free saltwater pool with a swim-up bar, free Wi-Fi in the square, and cantinas. Useful if anyone wants out of the spa loop early.

Conexión

México notes

The handful of things that matter.

  • USD works everywhere at cruise ports. Small bills (1s, 5s, 10s). Change comes back in pesos. For better exchange, Banorte ATM in town gives pesos. optional.
  • Taxis at cruise ports run on posted fixed rates. verifying the rate before getting in is smart. "For the car, all of us, that's the total?" works.
  • Tap water: avoidable. Bottled is everywhere and cheap. Restaurant ice is fine; they use purified water.
  • Reef-safe sunscreen required by law in protected areas. Sun Bum, Stream2Sea, Thinksport.
  • Vendors on the malecón are common. "No gracias" with a smile, keep walking.
  • "Free taxi / breakfast / tequila" at the Cozumel pier exit = 90-min timeshare pitch. Skippable.
  • Pharmacies at cruise ports sell legit discounted meds.
  • Cash plan USD pp in small bills covers taxi, beach club credits, food, mezcal.
